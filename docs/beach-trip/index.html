Where it lands

The honest read

The single biggest lever isn't the hotel — it's the dates. Shifting the November week a few days earlier, to Nov 17–22, cuts the O'Hare nonstops ~$150–380/person and often the room too. It beats both the Nov 20–25 baseline and a March trip for every destination.

Cheapest: Corazón Cabo & Fairmont Mayakoba tie at ~$3,020 (Nov 17–22) — both swimmable, both nonstop.

Closest to Montage vibe: Conrad Punta de Mita, ~$4,330 (Nov 17–22) — design-forward, calm cove, kids' club.

Your wife's Montage point checks out: in the Nov 17–22 window the Montage room is ~$914/night and the whole trip is ~$7,090, not ~$9,040. Still the priciest of the swimmable picks, but much closer.

On the three you asked me to add: Hyatt Ziva is the easy all-inclusive family play (~$4,680) but not à la carte. The Cape and Nobu are gorgeous, but both sit on non-swimmable surf/Pacific beaches — and The Cape is couples-oriented — so they fight your "pool AND swimmable ocean, family-friendly" brief. Priced anyway so you can judge.

Is New Year's week (Dec 26–31) better? No — it's the priciest window of all: nonstops roughly double, Montage & Conrad are sold out, and rooms carry a holiday premium.
